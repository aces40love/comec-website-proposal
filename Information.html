Our Organization

The Commission on Missing & Exploited Children (COMEC) is a nonprofit organization based in Memphis, Tennessee. Established to address the critical needs of families impacted by missing and exploited children, COMEC serves as a vital resource connecting families with law enforcement, providing support services, and driving community education initiatives across the state.

We work in partnership with local, state, and federal agencies to advocate for stronger protections, improved response protocols, and comprehensive prevention programs. All operations are guided by our commitment to professionalism, compassion, and measurable impact.

Our History

1984
FOUNDING

COMEC was founded in 1984 in response to the increasing number of missing children cases in the Memphis area and across the nation. Inspired by national efforts following the disappearance of children like Adam Walsh and the establishment of the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC), local law enforcement, community leaders, and concerned families came together to create a dedicated commission.

The organization initially focused on awareness campaigns, family support hotlines, and collaboration with police departments to improve search and recovery efforts. Over the decades, COMEC has evolved to include digital safety education, AMBER Alert advocacy, prevention training in schools, and legislative advocacy.

Key Milestones:
• 1984: Official incorporation in Tennessee
• 1990s: Instrumental in statewide AMBER Alert implementation
• 2005: Expanded programs to include internet safety workshops
• 2015-Present: Focus on leadership development and community partnerships
• 2026: New Executive Leadership under Greg Bethel and President Philip Boals

Continued Legacy

For over forty years, COMEC has remained a steadfast advocate for Tennessee's children and families. Our history is one of resilience, adaptation to new threats (particularly online exploitation), and unwavering commitment to the principle that no child should be forgotten and no family should face these challenges alone.
