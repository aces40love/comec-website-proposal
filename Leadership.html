New Leadership Announcement

As announced in the official COMEC Press Release, we are pleased to welcome our new Executive Director and President of the Board of Directors.

Gregory Bethel

Executive Director

Gregory Bethel has been appointed as the new Executive Director of COMEC — The Commission on Missing and Exploited Children. A dynamic leader with more than 20 years of experience, Greg brings a proven track record in corporate leadership, executive training, stakeholder engagement, and community outreach.

Greg was elected to his position after Len K. Edwards retired from the position in April 2026, after 25 years of service. As COMEC's longest-tenured board member and a former Vice President, Greg steps into this role with both purpose and passion.

In his current role as HRBP Manager for the City of Memphis, he has developed a strong understanding of the community. His previous experience as Director of Human Resources for a large school district and Store Operations Manager has prepared him well.

Looking ahead, Greg is focused on advancing COMEC's mission through strategic fundraising, enhanced donor relations, deeper stakeholder engagement, and building impactful partnerships for long-term success.

Appointed 2025 • Dedicated to child advocacy and community leadership

Philip Boals

President of the Board

Philip Boals has been elected President of the Board of Directors for COMEC. He brings nearly three decades of professional experience in real estate valuation, banking, finance, land surveying, development consulting, and organizational leadership.

Throughout his career, Philip has built a reputation for thoughtful decision making, practical problem solving, and a steady leadership style grounded in accountability, sound judgment, and clear communication.

His professional background includes commercial real estate valuation, collateral review, asset management, regulatory compliance, and the development of internal policies and procedures. He has contributed to strategic planning and operational improvement projects.

As President, Philip is focused on strengthening COMEC's public presence, expanding community partnerships, improving fundraising efforts, and ensuring the organization's long-term sustainability. He is committed to working with leadership, board members, and partners to support the vital mission of advocacy, prevention, and family support.

Elected 2025 • Real estate professional and nonprofit leader

About the Transition

These leadership changes follow the retirement of long-time Executive Director Len K. Edwards. The Commission is grateful for his 25 years of dedicated service and looks forward to this new chapter under the guidance of Greg Bethel and Philip Boals.
