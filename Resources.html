Internet Safety for Families

Practical steps to protect children online

Key Recommendations

  • Have open conversations about online risks with your children.
  • Use parental control tools and privacy settings on all devices.
  • Monitor app usage and establish clear family technology rules.
  • Teach children to recognize grooming, phishing, and cyberbullying.
  • Keep computers in common areas and set time limits for internet use.

Runaway Prevention & Response

Strategies for families and communities

Warning Signs

  • Frequent arguments or withdrawal from family
  • Declining school performance or truancy
  • New friends with negative influence
  • Signs of depression, anxiety, or substance use

What To Do

  • Contact the National Runaway Safeline: 1-800-RUNAWAY
  • File a police report immediately — do not wait 24 hours
  • Reach out to family, friends, and known associates
  • Utilize COMEC support services for family mediation
Hotline: National Center for Missing & Exploited Children: 1-800-THE-LOST (1-800-843-5678)

Understanding AMBER Alerts

How the system works and how to respond

AMBER Alerts are emergency notifications issued when law enforcement determines that a child has been abducted and is in imminent danger. The acronym stands for America's Missing: Broadcast Emergency Response.

Criteria for an AMBER Alert:

  • Confirmed child abduction
  • Child is 17 or younger
  • Child is at risk of serious injury or death
  • Enough descriptive information available to aid in recovery

How to Respond to an AMBER Alert:

  1. Look at the description of the child, abductor, and vehicle
  2. If you see something, call 911 immediately — do not approach
  3. Share the alert through social media responsibly
  4. Remain vigilant until the child is recovered
